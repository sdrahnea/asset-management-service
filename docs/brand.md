A brand record in an assessment‑management system usually needs to capture **identity, ownership, market presence, legal protection, and performance signals**. Brands are multidimensional assets, so the information model is broader than for bank accounts or bonds.

The sections below outline the essential and optional data points used in professional brand‑management, marketing‑analytics, and valuation systems.

---

# 🏷️ Core Identity Information
These fields define what the brand *is* at its most basic level.

- **Brand name**
- **Logo and visual identifiers** (stored as references, not raw files)
- **Tagline / slogan**
- **Brand description** (short and long versions)
- **Brand category** (e.g., food, electronics, fashion)
- **Parent company / owner**
- **Country of origin**

A structured identity layer helps with classification, search, and reporting.

---

# 🧩 Legal & Ownership Attributes
Brands are legally protected assets, so your system should track:

- **Trademark registration number**
- **Trademark type** (wordmark, figurative, combined)
- **Jurisdictions covered**
- **Registration date / expiration date**
- **Status** (active, expired, pending, disputed)
- **Associated legal documents** (stored as references)

This layer is essential if your assessments involve compliance, IP risk, or brand integrity.

---

# 📣 Market Presence & Positioning
These fields describe how the brand is positioned in the market.

- **Industry / sector**
- **Target audience**
- **Brand positioning statement**
- **Value proposition**
- **Competitors**
- **Geographic markets served**

This information supports competitive analysis and strategic assessments.

---

# 📊 Performance & Perception Indicators
Assessment systems often include metrics that reflect brand strength.

- **Brand equity score** (internal or external model)
- **Brand awareness** (aided/unaided)
- **Brand sentiment** (from surveys or social listening)
- **Market share**
- **Customer loyalty indicators**
- **Net Promoter Score (NPS)**

These metrics help quantify brand health over time.

---

# 🌐 Digital Footprint
Modern brands are evaluated heavily through their online presence.

- **Official website**
- **Social media accounts** (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, etc.)
- **Follower counts**
- **Engagement metrics**
- **SEO visibility indicators**

This layer is useful for digital‑first assessments.

---

# 🧾 Operational Metadata
Useful for internal tracking and lifecycle management.

- **Date added to the system**
- **Last updated**
- **Assessment history**
- **Assigned analyst / reviewer**
- **Tags / classification labels**

This helps maintain clean data and auditability.

---

# 🧮 Optional Financial Attributes
If your assessment service includes valuation or financial scoring:

- **Revenue attributed to the brand**
- **Profitability metrics**
- **Licensing income**
- **Brand valuation (if available)**
- **Cost of brand maintenance**

These are common in brand valuation frameworks like Interbrand or BrandZ.

---

# 🗂️ Example Brand Data Model (High-Level)

| Category | Key Fields |
|---------|------------|
| Identity | name, logo reference, description, category |
| Legal | trademark number, jurisdiction, status |
| Market | positioning, competitors, target audience |
| Performance | equity score, sentiment, market share |
| Digital | website, social accounts, engagement |
| Financial | revenue, valuation, licensing |
| Metadata | tags, assessment history, reviewer |
